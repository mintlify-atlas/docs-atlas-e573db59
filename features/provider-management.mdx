Provider Management maintains the directory and credentials of medical providers in network. It is the authoritative record for every physician, doctor, and clinical professional that your organization works with, ensuring that only credentialed, in-network providers are associated with patient enrollments and clinical files.

## What Provider Management Stores

Navigating to **ENROLLMENT → Provider Management** (path `#provider-management`) opens the provider directory view. The description rendered in the dashboard reads:

> *Directory and credentials of medical providers and doctors in network.*

Each provider record holds the identity and credentialing data needed to verify that a provider is authorized to participate in the enrollment workflow.

## Adding a Provider

A prominent **Add Provider** button is available at the top of the Provider Management view. Use it to register a new medical provider in the directory.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Provider Management">
    In the left sidebar, navigate to **ENROLLMENT → Provider Management**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Add Provider">
    Select the **Add Provider** button to open the provider registration form.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Complete the provider details">
    Fill in the required provider information — including name, credentials, and network affiliation — then submit to add the record to the directory.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Role in the Enrollment Workflow

<Note>
  Provider Management is a core part of the enrollment workflow. Client accounts (configured in [Client Management](/features/client-management)) reference in-network providers, and accurate provider credentials are required before a provider can be linked to an active patient enrollment.
</Note>

Keeping provider records current ensures that credentialing checks performed during enrollment are accurate and that clinical files reflect the correct provider associations. Always verify that a provider's credentials are up to date before associating them with a new client or patient enrollment.
